Tampon Tax Across The US






Across the United States, the majority of the states implement a tampon tax.

49%

That is the percentage of women in the United States who experience period poverty. Due to the costs of menstrual products, each woman is expected to pay about $6,000 dollars in their lifetime on essential menstruation services. On top of this high cost, many states in the US implement a tampon tax that puts a tax on these menstrual products, forcing women to pay even more money. This disproportionately affects impoverished women, as due to their lack of money, they do not experience the same healthy lifestyle as their male counterparts, vastly decreasing the opportunities these women have. Period poverty is real, and tampon taxes only exemplify it.
